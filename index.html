Professional Background

I've worked in several different industries ranging from Education, Healthcare, Landscaping, and more recently, Energy.

I recently left my full-time role as a Lead Pipeline Integrity Analyst at T.D.Williamson. I managed a team of Data Analysts working to prevent oil and gas pipelines from leaking, causing environmental damage and loss of property or life. This short video provides an introduction to pipeline integrity and how a "smart pig" collects data from a pipeline. The "pig" returns magnetic flux leakage data for analysts to review for possible integrity threats.


Why Software Engineering?

Experiencing the power of programming inspired me to pursue a career change.

I've spent the last 6 months learning Python to help automate parts of my job. Finding automation opportunities quickly became my favorite part of the day. I was extremely excited and passionate about one project in particular. The application I created would display pipeline integrity statistics for the user. This helped our department understand important details about a pipeline that would otherwise take hours to gather all the data for. After successfully implementing my application to our department, I decided to pursue software development full-time.


Here iss some additional reasons why I want a career in software development:

  • In high demand
  • I love problem solving
  • I enjoy a good challenge

Saying Goodbye to Friends at Work

In software engineering, "rubber duck debugging" is a method of debugging code by articulating a problem in spoken or written language. After leaving my job on Friday, my team came up with a clever goodbye gift. Each member of the team wrote a goodbye message on a little rubber duck, wishing me the best of luck.
